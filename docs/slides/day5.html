Today's superpower

Rematches are science

The Arena is deterministic: same start → same result, every time. So when you change your brain and rematch, any improvement is real, not luck.

Train → save → rematch → keep what helped. That's the loop.

The recipe that won 🏆

Teach → Evolve vs the real opponent

Teach get competent fast → Evolve that brain against the exact bot you must beat.

Of every combo we tried on real devices, this was the only one that won — 5–2. Each generation pushes your good brain toward the actual goal.

⚠️ The trap next to it

Training more can make it worse

Take a good distilled striker, then…Result
nothing (Teach only)competent — a one-goal game
Evolve vs the real opponentwins 5–2 ✅
Q-Learn vs a still coneregresses to 0–7 ⚠️

The cone is a simpler game than the real match — its reward overwrote the good habits.

🔧 The fix is realism, not a new trick

Train against what you'll actually face

  • Q-Learn vs the real moving opponent → recovered 0–7 → 3–3.
  • Let the reward run finish — a half-done pass is stuck mid-regression. Finished, it flipped a draw into a win.

Over-fitting 🥶

Evolve too long on one fixed pitch and the bot “just stays there” in a real match — it memorised that board.

Fix: vary the board while training. (Sound familiar? It's the Day-2 maze lesson again — a memoriser fails on the unseen.)

The loop that levels you up

Lost? Train against the winner

  1. Save the foe — it copies the winner into your library.
  2. Train against that exact bot.
  3. Rematch (it's deterministic — a fair test).

Caveat: a big gap can need more than one pass.

🩺 “My bot got worse!”

That's the best lesson, not a fail

  • Refined vs a too-easy partner (a still cone)? → use a real moving foe.
  • Stopped the reward run early? → let it finish.
  • Evolved long on one board? → vary the board.
  • Still stuck? → re-Teach a fresh base, then refine.
